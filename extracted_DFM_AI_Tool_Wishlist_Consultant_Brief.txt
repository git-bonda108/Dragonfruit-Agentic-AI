DRAGONFRUIT MEDIA
AI Tool Development Wish List
March 2026

Context 
The tools described in this document represent team-identified opportunities where AI/automation could meaningfully reduce manual effort, improve quality, or accelerate delivery timelines. Each request originates from the practitioners who do this work daily.
Tools are organized by role. Priority levels reflect organizational impact and urgency. We are looking for recommendations on whether to build bespoke solutions, configure existing platforms, or combine off-the-shelf tools to meet each need.

1. Creative Director Tools
Creative Directors drive the content strategy, packaging, and creative vision for each pod. They currently spend significant time on manual competitor research, ideation, and subjective quality assessments that could be augmented or partially automated.

2. Editor / PPM Tools
Editors represent the largest headcount in the organization (~60 people) and account for approximately 65% of all logged production time. Even small per-editor efficiency gains compound significantly at scale.

3. Producer Tools
Producers manage client relationships, timelines, and cross-functional coordination. Their time is heavily taxed by administrative overhead and communication management.

Summary & Prioritization Matrix
The table below consolidates all 16 requested tools by priority tier for scoping and budgeting purposes.

High Priority (8 tools)
These tools address the most time-intensive manual processes or the highest-impact strategic needs. They should be scoped and prototyped first.
Creative Director: CD-1 (Predictive Retention), CD-2 (Outlier Ideation), CD-3 (Visual Packaging Intelligence), CD-4 (A/B Test Monitor)
Editor: ED-1 (Frame.io Sentiment), ED-2 (Automated QC), ED-3 (Capacity Assignment)
Producer: PR-4 (Claude-to-ClickUp Agent)

Medium Priority (8 tools)
These tools provide meaningful efficiency gains and quality improvements but have lower urgency or narrower scope.
Creative Director: CD-5 (Packaging Scorer), CD-6 (Client Knowledge Bases), CD-7 (Script Voice Matching), CD-8 (Slack Scoring Bot), CD-9 (Client Outlier Aggregator)
Producer: PR-1 (Timeline Calculator), PR-2 (Client Sentiment Tracker), PR-3 (Slack Templates)

Notes for the Consultant
Our primary project management platform is ClickUp. Our primary review platform is Frame.io. Internal communication runs through Slack. Any tools built should integrate with these systems.
We are open to a mix of approaches: bespoke tools where off-the-shelf solutions don't exist, configured SaaS products where they do, and lightweight automations (Zapier, Make, custom bots) for integration glue.
For each tool, we would like your proposal to include: recommended approach (build vs. buy vs. configure), estimated development timeline, ongoing maintenance requirements, and approximate cost range.
We are particularly interested in understanding which of these tools can share underlying infrastructure (e.g., a common data pipeline for competitor monitoring could serve CD-2, CD-3, CD-4, and CD-9).

# | Tool Name | Description & Requirements | Priority | Category
CD-1 | Predictive Retention Scoring | A tool that accepts a full script as input and outputs a predicted audience retention curve, similar to YouTube Analytics retention graphs. The model should identify likely drop-off points, flag sections with low engagement potential, and suggest structural edits. Think of it as an AI focus group that simulates viewer behavior before a video is produced. Input: script text, channel context, video format (shortform/longform). Output: retention curve prediction, drop-off risk flags, improvement suggestions with rationale. | HIGH | Strategy
CD-2 | Competitive Outlier Ideation Engine | A research tool that continuously monitors a curated list of approximately 20 competitor channels per client and surfaces outlier-performing content. The tool should triangulate across three dimensions: (1) content themes that are gaining disproportionate traction, (2) title formats and linguistic patterns correlated with high performance, and (3) emerging topic clusters before they saturate. The system should distinguish between a competitor's baseline performance and genuine outliers. Input: list of competitor channel URLs. Output: weekly digest of outlier videos with analysis of why they outperformed, categorized by theme, format, and timing patterns. | HIGH | Research
CD-3 | Visual Packaging & Thumbnail Intelligence | An extension of CD-2 focused specifically on the visual dimension. The tool should analyze competitor thumbnails and surface insights on composition, color palette, facial expression patterns, text overlay strategies, and visual tension/curiosity techniques. Additionally, given a script, it should extract the most counterintuitive or curiosity-gap-inducing phrases and suggest them as thumbnail text candidates. Cross-reference with Google Trends/SEO data to validate which concepts have search demand. Useful for channel-level shelf strategy (how thumbnails look as a collection on the channel page). | HIGH | Creative
CD-4 | Competitor A/B Test Monitor | An automated surveillance tool that detects when competitors change titles, thumbnails, or descriptions (A/B testing). When a change coincides with a performance spike, the system should automatically alert the Creative Director via Slack DM with the before/after comparison and performance delta. This replaces the current manual process of periodically checking competitor channels for changes. | HIGH | Intelligence
CD-5 | Packaging Tension Scorer | A scoring tool that evaluates the strength of a title + thumbnail combination. The tool should rate packaging on a defined scale (e.g., 1-10) across dimensions like curiosity gap, emotional trigger strength, clarity of value proposition, and click-worthiness. Ideally delivered in a playful "roast" format that provides memorable, actionable feedback. Could function as a Slack command (post a title + thumbnail image, get back a score and critique). | MEDIUM | Quality
CD-6 | Client-Specific Knowledge Bases | Dedicated Claude Projects or equivalent LLM workspaces, one per client, pre-loaded with that client's brand voice documentation, past scripts, performance data, audience demographics, and style guides. The Creative Director should be able to query this workspace conversationally to get on-brand suggestions, reference past decisions, and maintain consistency. We may need a tutorial or setup guide for the team to configure and maintain these. Alternatively, this could be a Notion-integrated or Slack-integrated bot. | MEDIUM | Workflow
CD-7 | AI-Assisted Script Voice Matching | A tool that takes a draft script and rewrites or adjusts it to match a specific client's voice and delivery style. The system should be trained on (or prompted with) examples of the client's previous scripts, verbal cadences, vocabulary preferences, and personality. This is partially addressed by tools like Subscriber.ai but may need customization. | MEDIUM | Creative
CD-8 | Slack-Based Predictive Scoring Bot | A Slack integration where a Creative Director can post a proposed title, intro hook, or packaging concept and receive an instant predictive score (1-10) estimating likely performance. The model should incorporate channel-specific historical data to calibrate predictions. This is related to CD-1 and CD-5 but optimized for quick, lightweight checks during the ideation phase rather than full script analysis. | MEDIUM | Workflow
CD-9 | Client-Specific Outlier Aggregator | A per-client dashboard that aggregates outlier content from that client's competitive set and niche, tailored to their specific audience and content category. Unlike CD-2 (which is a general competitive tool), this produces client-contextualized insights: "Here's what's working in your niche this week and how it maps to your content calendar." | MEDIUM | Research

ED-1 | Frame.io Comment Sentiment Analyzer | An AI agent that continuously monitors Frame.io review comments in real time and produces two outputs: (1) a video-level sentiment analysis summarizing the overall tone and satisfaction level of the reviewer's feedback, and (2) a client sentiment tracker that aggregates feedback patterns over time per client to identify satisfaction trends, recurring complaints, or escalation risks. ED-2 | Automated Video QC Checker | A tool that reviews a rendered video export against a client-specific checklist and produces a timestamped error report. The checklist would include items such as: correct intro/outro branding, lower-third accuracy, audio levels within spec, color grading consistency, required b-roll placements, caption accuracy, and export settings compliance. The tool should flag violations with timestamps and severity ratings, reducing the manual QC burden on Post-Production Managers and preventing revision cycles caused by missed checklist items. | HIGH | Quality
ED-3 | Automated Capacity-Based Task Assignment | An integration between ClickUp (our project management tool) and editor availability data that automatically assigns incoming editing tasks to editors with available capacity. The system should factor in current workload, estimated task duration, editor skill match (e.g., shortform vs. longform specialization), and pod assignment. This replaces the current manual process where Production Coordinators check availability and assign tasks by hand. | HIGH | Workflow

The solution in plain language

This section explains what you have today, what we add, and how the layers work — so the diagram and the rest of the proposal make sense to everyone.

What you have today

Dragonfruit operates at scale: 90+ team members and 60+ videos per month across clients. Your pipeline runs from client onboarding through pre-production, production, post-production, publishing, and monthly reporting. Each pod has a Producer, Creative Director, Production Coordinator, and Post Production Manager (who manages 10–12 Editors); you also have shared Scriptwriters, Thumbnail Designers, and Data Analysts. You use ClickUp for tasks and batches, Slack for internal and client communication, Notion for docs and SOPs, Frame.io for review, and Dropbox for footage — plus Make, Zapier, and the rest of your tool stack (~$12.8K/month, 40+ subscriptions). Your time audit and dream allocations show where time goes today and where you want it to go; your AI survey (73 people across 5 roles) and wish list spell out the tools and workflows you want. Nothing in this proposal invents data: we use only what’s in your materials.

What we’re adding (and what we’re not)

We are not replacing ClickUp, Slack, Notion, Frame.io, or Dropbox. We are adding a layer in between your team and those tools. That layer does three things, in line with your brief:

  1. Workflow automation (primary lever) — Automate repetitive tasks across ClickUp, Slack, Notion, and Dropbox so the bulk of coordination overhead shrinks (e.g. task creation, status updates, templated messages).
  2. Agentic infrastructure (secondary lever) — AI agents that handle task routing, status updates, feedback distribution, and content-prep workflows. Each agent is aligned to a role (Producer, PC, CD, PPM) and only does what you’ve defined; at critical steps you approve before anything is created, sent, or assigned.
  3. AI wish list toolkit by role (enabler) — Right-fit AI tools per role from your wish list (e.g. predictive retention for CDs, automated QC for PPMs, Claude-to-ClickUp for Producers), so you move toward your dream time allocations.

Human-in-the-loop: Task creation, assignment, and client-facing messages never go out without a human approval step. You stay in control; the system suggests and you confirm.

What the four layers in the diagram mean

The diagram below has four layers, top to bottom:

  • Layer 1 — Where work comes in: The same places it does today: Slack, ClickUp, Frame.io, Notion, Dropbox, and your team (Producer, PC, CD, PPM). We don’t change how work enters; we plug into these.
  • Layer 2 — What we add (AI by role): Role-based AI that understands your workflow. Producer Agent helps create and route tasks; PC Agent helps with status and client comms; CD Agent helps with retention, competitor intel, and packaging; PPM Agent helps with QC and who to assign. At each critical action, you approve (e.g. “confirm task,” “approve client message,” “confirm assignee”).
  • Layer 3 — The wiring: Connectors we build so the AI can talk safely to ClickUp, Slack, Notion, Frame.io, and your QC/assignment logic. This is the technical “plumbing”; your team doesn’t need to manage it day to day.
  • Layer 4 — Your existing systems: ClickUp, Slack, Notion, Frame.io, Make/Zapier — unchanged. We read from and write to them only where you’ve agreed, and only after you’ve approved the action.

Together, these layers support your objective: scale from 4 to 7 clients per pod by EOY 2026 without a proportional headcount increase, by reducing coordination overhead and giving each role the right AI support.

B.0 Market context — who else does this?

Several players offer parts of what you need, but none deliver the full pipeline + role-based wish list you described:

  • Generic automation (e.g. Lindy, Zapier/Make): Slack↔ClickUp templates, ~$50–300/month. Good for simple triggers; they do not provide predictive retention, Frame.io sentiment, automated QC, capacity-based assignment, or CD-specific tools (outlier ideation, A/B monitor).
  • ClickUp-focused agents (e.g. UpSys, boutique shops): Custom AI agents for task creation, triage, onboarding. Typically custom-priced; strong on task automation but not on creative/post-production (retention, QC, packaging, sentiment).
